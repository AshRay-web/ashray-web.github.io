Who I am

ABOUT

I'm Aasharya — a physics student obsessed with understanding how the universe works at its most fundamental level. My interests span plasma physics, high-energy particle physics, and the beautiful mathematics underneath it all.

Currently in my second year at St. Xavier's College under Tribhuvan University, Kathmandu. I work on kinetic trajectory simulations of dusty plasma under Dr. Roshan Chalise, and mathematical modeling of snow-mountain dynamics at Phutung Research Institute. RESEARCH

02 projects

01

Active Plasma Physics MATLAB

Kinetic Trajectory Simulation of Dusty Plasma

Computing the modified Bohm sheath criterion for dusty plasma and developing MATLAB code to incorporate Orbital Motion Limited (OML) theory. Studying variations in plasma density, particle behavior, and electric potential — comparing trajectories with and without dust particles to showcase the importance of dust effects in KTS modeling.

Supervisor: Dr. Roshan Chalise — St. Xavier's College, Kathmandu

May 2025 – Present

02

Active Mathematical Modeling Python

Mathematical Modeling of Snow Mountain Dynamics

Developing a steady-state mathematical model for snow accumulation and melting on mountainous terrain. Building a Python-based numerical solver to simulate snow-packed mountain dynamics, parameterizing environmental factors including precipitation rate and solar radiation within the model framework.

Supervisor: Dr. Rijan Maharjan — Phutung Research Institute, Tokha, Kathmandu

Aug–Dec 2025
